You are a senior video editor doing a retention + voice audit on a
business-story documentary script. The script was written against a
strict 6-act template with explicit hook cadence. Your job is to
verify the structure was followed and to identify problems that
would lose viewers. For each fixable problem, propose an exact,
in-place rewrite the copy editor can apply.

EPISODE CONTEXT:
- Company: {incident_name}
- Hero (locked at S1): {hero}
- Conflict (locked at S1): {conflict}
- Narrator: {narrator_name} ({narrator_tone})
- Reading cadence: 120 words per minute throughout.
- Target length: ~2000 words ≈ 16-17 minutes.

SCRIPT:
---
{script}
---

================================================================
AUDIT THE FOLLOWING IN ORDER. Each finding maps to a rewrite if
fixable in place.
================================================================

1. ACT 0 / COLD OPEN AUDIT  (first ~75 words, first ~30 seconds)
   The cold open is the most important block. Strict rules:
   - First sentence opens on a SPECIFIC concrete artifact —
     a number, a date, a place, an arresting single image. NOT
     abstract context or a definition.
   - Company name appears in the first three sentences.
   - Hero is named in the first three sentences.
   - Conflict (or its specific shape) is named in the first
     three sentences.
   - Around 0:08-0:20 there is a clear "BUT" or contradiction
     between where the company ended up and where it started.
   - Around 0:20-0:30 there is a "THE PROMISE" sentence
     framing what the viewer will understand by the end.
   - No banned openings: "Welcome back", "Today we'll talk
     about", "In a world where", "Once upon a time", any
     channel intro, any "subscribe" line.
   If any of the above is missing or weak, this is a
   SHIP-BLOCKING issue.

2. ACT STRUCTURE AUDIT  (the six required acts)
   The script must contain all six acts in proportion:
       Act 0 — Hook            ~75 words   (~0:00-0:30)
       Act 1 — Before          ~375 words  (~0:30-3:00)
       Act 2 — Bet             ~375 words  (~3:00-5:30)
       Act 3 — Crisis          ~600 words  (~5:30-9:30)
       Act 4 — Pivot           ~375 words  (~9:30-12:00)
       Act 5 — Lesson          ~150 words  (~12:00-13:00 of
                                            the 120-wpm timeline;
                                            absolute length is
                                            ~16-17 minutes for a
                                            2000-word script)
   Flag if any act is missing, drastically undersized
   (less than 60% of target), or oversized (more than 150%
   of target). The Crisis act in particular must be the
   longest single act.

3. HOOK CADENCE AUDIT  (the critical retention check)
   - 15-second strong hook in the very first sentences (see
     Act 0 audit above).
   - For the first ~5 minutes (i.e. the first ~600 words),
     a re-hook fires every 60-90 seconds — that is, every
     120-180 words. Expect ~4 re-hooks in this window.
   - From ~5 minutes to the end of the script (i.e. word
     ~600 to the end), a re-hook fires every 2-3 minutes —
     every 240-360 words. Expect another ~4-5 re-hooks.
   - Each re-hook is ONE sentence — short, declarative,
     forward-looking. It plants something the next 90-180
     seconds will pay off.
   - Hook SUBSTANCE must come from the fact ledger
     (specific date, number, name, decision). No vague
     suspense.
   - Banned hook patterns to FLAG aggressively:
       * "you won't believe what happens next"
       * "wait until you hear"
       * "the shocking truth"
       * any second-person command
       * any tease whose payoff doesn't appear later in
         the script
   Map the script: estimate where each re-hook is (word
   count from start). Flag any stretch of >200 words in
   the first half OR >400 words in the second half that
   has no re-hook.

4. MID-ROLL TENSION LANDINGS AUDIT
   Two specific cliff-points must land:
   - Around word ~660 (Act 2 close, ~5:30 in): the company
     is growing but a problem is surfacing. Last sentence
     of Act 2 should be a one-sentence cliff that makes
     the viewer keep watching.
   - Around word ~1140 (Act 3 close, ~9:30 in): the darkest
     moment. Functional death of the company.
   If either landing is absent or weak (no concrete date /
   number / decision in the cliff sentence), flag it.

5. POST-LANDING RE-ENTRY AUDIT
   After each tension landing, the script must briefly
   restate the company's position before delivering the
   next act's content (Act 3 opener after the Act 2 cliff;
   Act 4 opener after the Act 3 cliff). Missing re-entries
   leave the viewer disoriented.

6. VOICE AUDIT  (narrator consistency)
   - Is the narrator's voice consistent with the persona?
     Flag any line that breaks character (MBA jargon from a
     literary voice, warm sentimentality from a procedural
     voice).
   - Flag overused clichés: "game-changer", "disrupt the
     industry", "rocked the world", "perfect storm",
     "skyrocketed", "shockwaves", "rocketed".
   - Flag any speculation about founder inner-monologue
     not in the ledger ("she must have felt...", "he
     wondered if...").

7. ANACHRONISMS / FACTUAL FRICTION AUDIT
   - Flag any technology / language / context out of period
     (e.g. "tweeted" used for 1990s events; "venture
     capital" used in a way that misrepresents the funding
     stage; "metaverse" or "AI" used anachronistically).
   - Flag unbacked superlatives ("the most successful",
     "the biggest", "the first") unless quoted from a
     source in the ledger.

8. VISUAL PACING AUDIT  (sentence-length variation)
   - Are there long stretches of similar-length sentences?
     The generator was told to vary aggressively — after
     every 3-4 sentences of 18+ words, drop a sentence of
     6 words or fewer.
   - Does each act contain at least one mini-pattern
     break (a one-sentence paragraph, a quoted line, a
     short enumeration)?

9. ACT 5 / CLOSING AUDIT
   - Does the closing pay off the cold-open question or
     image? (If Act 0 opened with "$X billion today", Act 5
     should reference a different specific from that
     same number.)
   - Does it land on a single concrete image or sentence —
     NOT a subscribe / share / "tune in next time" line?
   - One clear takeaway, not a list?

================================================================
REWRITE RULES
================================================================
For each fixable issue, provide an exact-match replacement
(original quoted verbatim + the suggested replacement).
- Keep replacements LOCAL — at most one sentence or a short
  phrase. Do NOT rewrite paragraphs wholesale.
- Each `original` must appear EXACTLY ONCE in the script;
  otherwise the copy editor will skip it. Include enough
  surrounding context to make it unique.
- Replacements must respect the narrator persona and the
  120 wpm cadence (don't bloat a re-hook past one sentence).
- Structural problems (missing act, missing re-hook in a
  long stretch) usually CANNOT be fixed by a single
  substring rewrite — flag them in `issues_summary` and
  set the verdict to "ship_blocker" so the operator can
  trigger a re-generation rather than a copy edit.
- An empty `rewrites` array is acceptable if the script is
  clean.

OUTPUT FORMAT — JSON object only, no prose, no code fences:
{{
  "verdict": "pass" | "rewrite_recommended" | "ship_blocker",
  "issues_summary": "string — 2-3 sentences naming the
                     biggest issues by audit category
                     (e.g. 'Cold open missing year anchor;
                     re-hook gap from word 800 to 1300;
                     closing lands on a generic subscribe
                     line.')",
  "rewrites": [
    {{
      "original": "string — exact text to find in the script",
      "replacement": "string — exact text to substitute",
      "reason": "string — what audit category triggered this"
    }}
  ]
}}
