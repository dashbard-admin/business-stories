Below is a business-story script with ## BEAT N ## markers. For each
beat, produce a visual plan entry for a comic-book-styled
documentary frame.

Each entry has:

- beat_id: BEAT_NN (zero-padded to 2 digits, e.g. "BEAT_01")
- estimated_seconds: word_count / 2.08  (i.e. ~125 wpm slow doc)
- visual_intent: one of [
    founder_portrait,
    office_environment,
    product_reveal,
    boardroom_meeting,
    street_scene,
    crowd_or_market,
    factory_or_workshop,
    document_or_headline,
    chart_abstraction,
    courtroom_scene,
    montage_panel
  ]
- specific_visual_description: 30-60 words describing what should be
  on screen, in comic-book panel terms — composition, pose, lighting,
  era markers, mood. NOT a photographic description.
- ken_burns_motion: one of [slow_zoom_in, slow_zoom_out,
  slow_pan_left, slow_pan_right, hold_still]
- public_domain_search_terms: 2-4 specific search queries that might
  find a real public-domain asset for this beat (Wikipedia, SEC
  filing screenshots, archive.org). Prefer searches that would
  actually return ATMOSPHERIC reference for the comic style — a
  storefront photo, a press-conference photo, a courtroom sketch —
  rather than the exact moment described.
- flux_fallback_prompt: if no public-domain asset is found, the
  FLUX prompt to generate the image. The caller will PREPEND the
  locked visual-style prefix and APPEND the negative prompt, so
  your prompt should be ONLY the scene description (no style
  language, no "no text", etc.).
- sfx_cue: optional, one of [typewriter, keyboard, phone_ring,
  applause, door_slam, traffic_hum, market_bell, newsprint_rustle,
  clock_tick, silence] or null. (Documentation only — NOT
  synthesised by this pipeline; the music bed runs uninterrupted.)

DO NOT echo the beat's prose back. The script text is already on
disk; the caller will join it back into each entry by beat_id. Keep
your output focused on the VISUAL plan and asset hints.

CRITICAL — FLUX CANNOT RENDER LEGIBLE TEXT.
The image generator (FLUX via CLI) reliably produces glyph-scribble
when asked for words, sentences, headlines, signage, brand logos
spelled out, page text, or any readable writing. Treat this as a
hard constraint.

Do NOT write prompts like:
- "a newspaper headline reading 'COMPANY COLLAPSES'"
- "a sign labeled 'Acme Headquarters'"
- "a storefront with the word NIKE on the awning"
- "a contract with the words 'TERMINATION CLAUSE' visible"

Instead, when the beat references a document, sign, headline, or
brand logo, frame it visually WITHOUT legible content:

- document_or_headline → "extreme close-up of a folded newspaper,
  print grain visible, headline letters deliberately out of focus,
  hands holding it at the edge of frame"
- a sign / storefront → "a brand storefront at oblique angle, awning
  shape and signature color (red, white) prominent, signage letters
  blurred or partial"
- a logo on a building → "a corporate facade with the iconic logo
  shape silhouetted against evening sky, no legible wordmark"
- a contract / filing → "close-up of a stack of legal papers, paper
  grain, a pen lying across them, text not in focus"
- a chart / graph → "an abstracted line graph rising or falling on a
  whiteboard or projector screen, the curve is the subject, axis
  labels are not in focus"

For BRAND identification, lean on:
- the colour palette of the brand (Nike's red+white, Apple's
  brushed silver, Theranos's black+gold)
- the shape of the logo (the Nike swoosh, the Apple silhouette, the
  Coca-Cola contour bottle)
- the architecture of the headquarters
- the type of product (vintage CRT monitor for 1990s Apple, a
  blood-draw machine for Theranos, a yellow-and-blue cardboard box
  for Blockbuster)

CRITICAL — DO NOT REQUEST RECOGNISABLE LIVING-CELEBRITY FACES.
For visual_intent=founder_portrait, prefer profile or
back-of-the-head shots, silhouettes, hand-on-document shots, or
oblique-angle compositions that imply identity through context.
FLUX renders specific living-celebrity faces poorly and the comic
style benefits from the visual restraint anyway.
