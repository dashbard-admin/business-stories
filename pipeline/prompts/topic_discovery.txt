AUDIENCE DEMAND (THIS IS WHAT KILLS MOST FACELESS CHANNELS):
A topic is not viable just because it is interesting. It must have
an existing audience signal:
- The company name should be searchable. Audiences who'd watch a
  business documentary should already be googling this company or
  founder. If the topic is too obscure ("a regional plumbing supply
  chain in 1962"), no one will search for it and the video will
  not surface. After your topic is proposed, an external system will
  query SearXNG / YouTube for `"<company> documentary"` and reject
  anything with fewer than 3 hits.
- The topic should NOT already be saturated to death. A few dozen
  competing videos is healthy demand. Hundreds means the niche is
  closed off to new entrants. The external system also rejects any
  topic returning more than 100 distinct video URLs. So: well-known
  enough that people search for it, but not so picked-over that the
  top results are all 100k-view explainers from 2019.
- Prefer companies/founders with RECENT NEWS ACTIVITY where
  applicable. A company that filed for bankruptcy in the last
  12-24 months is a goldmine — the news cycle drives traffic.
  Likewise a founder back in headlines, a scandal anniversary, a
  documentary release, a court ruling. Trending tailwinds matter.
