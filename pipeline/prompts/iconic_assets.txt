You are deriving a list of ICONIC VISUAL ASSETS that any documentary
about {company_name} must include in its panels — the visual cues
that make a viewer instantly recognise the company even at thumbnail
size, even with the sound off.

EPISODE:
- Company: {company_name}
- Founder/Protagonist: {founder}
- Year anchor: {year_anchor}
- Story kind: {story_kind}
- Hero: {hero}
- Conflict: {conflict}
- One-line pitch: {one_line_pitch}

================================================================
WHAT COUNTS AS AN ICONIC VISUAL ASSET
================================================================

Five categories. List the assets that EXIST AND ARE WELL-KNOWN for
this specific company in each category. If a category genuinely has
no iconic asset for this company, return an empty list for it —
don't invent.

  1. mascot_or_brand_character
     A recognisable cartoon / puppet / mascot specifically tied to
     the company. Examples: Pets.com sock puppet, Aflac duck,
     GEICO gecko, Tony the Tiger, Pillsbury Doughboy.
     Most companies have NO such mascot — return [] in that case.

  2. logo_or_wordmark
     The company's visual logo. Describe its shape, color, and any
     iconic associated object — NOT the legible wordmark itself
     (FLUX renders legible text poorly). Examples:
     "Apple rainbow apple silhouette with bite mark, six colored
      stripes" / "Nike swoosh shape, simple curved tick mark"
      / "McDonald's golden M arches".

  3. signature_products_or_objects
     The physical thing the company sold or made. For Pets.com:
     brown shipping boxes with the paw-print logo, bags of dog
     food. For Theranos: the Edison desktop blood-tester, glass
     vials, tiny finger-prick samples. For WeWork: pine-wood
     hot-desk furniture, neon "DO WHAT YOU LOVE" wall signs,
     glass conference-room walls.

  4. era_and_setting
     Visual signals of the time/place the story takes place.
     For a 1999-2000 dot-com story: beige CRT monitors, Windows 98
     desktops, AltaVista / early Netscape interfaces, Y2K-era
     office cubicles, dot-com-bubble Silicon Valley street scenes.
     For a 1980s story: brick mobile phones, neon office signs,
     IBM 5170 PCs, big-shoulder business attire.

  5. recurring_protagonist_appearance
     The founder/protagonist's visual signature — clothing,
     posture, accessories — that should appear consistently
     across panels. ONLY include items that are publicly
     documented (Wikipedia photos, news photos, documentary
     footage). Examples: "Elizabeth Holmes in a black turtleneck
     with blonde bun, deep voice affectation"; "Steve Jobs in
     black turtleneck and round wire-rimmed glasses, blue
     Levi's 501 jeans"; "Adam Neumann in t-shirt and bare feet,
     long dark hair, sometimes carrying a bottle of tequila in
     PR contexts".

================================================================
OUTPUT RULES
================================================================

- Each asset's `description` becomes a comma-separated phrase
  the FLUX prompt will use VERBATIM, so write it in the form
  a comic-book illustrator would actually use:
    "brown cardboard shipping boxes printed with the Pets.com
     paw-print logo and the address sticker on the side"
  NOT:
    "shipping boxes are sometimes seen at Pets.com fulfillment
     centres in early 2000."
- Keep each description ≤ 25 words.
- Avoid asking for LEGIBLE TEXT on the asset (FLUX renders text
  as glyph-scribble). For logos that ARE text-based (Pets.com is
  literally the word "Pets.com"), describe the visual SHAPE only:
  "stylized paw print and rounded sans-serif blue letterforms"
  rather than the literal string.
- Total assets across all categories: 3-8 items. Quality > quantity.
- If a category is irrelevant for this story (e.g. there's no
  mascot, no signature product because it's a service company),
  return an empty array for that category.

================================================================
OUTPUT FORMAT — JSON only, no prose, no code fences.
================================================================

{{
  "company_name": "{company_name}",
  "assets": [
    {{
      "category": "mascot_or_brand_character | logo_or_wordmark | signature_products_or_objects | era_and_setting | recurring_protagonist_appearance",
      "description": "string — illustrator-ready phrase, ≤ 25 words"
    }}
  ]
}}

If the company has NO iconic assets you can confidently document
(extremely rare — every well-known company has at least 2-3),
return:
  {{"company_name": "{company_name}", "assets": []}}
