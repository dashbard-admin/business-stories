HOOK CADENCE — STRICT RULES
================================================================
- A STRONG hook fires in the first 15 seconds (Act 0, lines 1-3).
- For the first 5 minutes, a re-hook fires every 60-90 seconds.
  At 120 wpm that means a re-hook roughly every 120-180 words.
- From 5 minutes to the end, a re-hook fires every 2-3 minutes,
  i.e. every 240-360 words.
- Each re-hook is exactly ONE sentence — short, declarative,
  forward-looking. It plants something the next 90-180 seconds
  will pay off.
- The substance of each hook MUST come from the fact ledger. No
  vague suspense, no "you won't believe what happens next", no
  second-person commands.

ACCEPTABLE HOOK PATTERNS (vary them — never repeat one):
  - Forward tease: "[Thing] was about to [happen] — and the
    person at the centre of it didn't yet know it."
  - Time-anchored:  "By [specific date], [specific number]
    had changed."
  - Character beat: "[Founder]'s next decision would cost
    [specific consequence]."
  - Industry zoom: "What was happening at [Company] would, in
    [N] years, be happening everywhere."
  - Documented quote: "[Person] later wrote: '[quote from ledger].'"

BANNED HOOK PATTERNS:
  - "You won't believe what happens next."
  - "Wait until you hear..."
  - "The shocking truth was..."
  - Any second-person command.
  - Any tease promising something not in the fact ledger.

================================================================
VISUAL PACING — AVOID MONOTONY
================================================================
Vary sentence length aggressively. Specifically:
- After every 3-4 sentences of 18+ words, drop a sentence of 6
  words or fewer. Use the short sentence to PUNCTUATE, not to
  pad.
- Each act should contain at least one mini-pattern break:
  - A one-sentence paragraph standing alone.
  - A direct quote from the ledger read verbatim.
  - A list of two or three concrete items (an enumeration,
    not a bullet list).
  - A rhetorical question THE NARRATOR THEN ANSWERS in the
    next sentence (use sparingly — once per script max).
- Beat markers (## BEAT N ##) align with visual scene changes,
  NOT with paragraph breaks. A beat is a new image. Group 2-3
  sentences of related content under one beat; switch beats
  when the scene logically changes (different person, different
  place, different timestamp, different object in focus).

================================================================
ABSOLUTE BANS (any of these → script gets rejected at S07)
================================================================
- Sensational openings ("What you are about to hear will shock you")
- Clickbait phrasings ("They never expected what happened next")
- AI disclaimers ("As an AI...")
- "Shocking", "unbelievable", "horrifying", "you won't believe",
  "game-changer", "disrupt the industry", "rocked the world",
  "perfect storm", "rocketed", "skyrocketed"
- "Tragic" more than twice in the whole script
- Rounding revenue / valuation up for drama
- Speculation about founder's inner monologue not in the ledger
- Made-up dialogue not present in the fact ledger
- Summary-style closings ("In conclusion...", "And so we have seen...")
- MBA-textbook jargon ("synergies", "value proposition",
  "stakeholder alignment") unless the source itself uses it
- Generic closers ("And the rest, as they say, is history.")
